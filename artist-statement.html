Artist Statement

Some words from me :)

The Gilded Age, a time in American history that was golden on the outside but rotten on the inside, began around the 1870s and ended around 1900. Riding off the high of the civil war, America was becoming a rising power. Fast. A strong government presence and high-spirited citizens promoted growth and the expansion of industries. The new hype surrounding America captured foreigners’ attention, drawing many to the growing country. However, it was not all sunshine and rainbows. There was little value placed on individualism, and while the country thrived many people did not. The Gilded Age was a time period of exploiting individuals while public morale turned against easy targets without targeting root issues.

Large corporations and business managers exploited workers for profit. Many unsafe business practices went unlooked due to corruption and the wealth in these new industries. Immigrants were rapidly coming into the country, providing an easy outlet to blame. Cities were overcrowded and densely populated with poor living conditions.

Industrialization

The goal of the industrialization page is to show its effect on the workers of the time and how it has created a standard of living that lasts even today. Chaplin’s piece was chosen to demonstrate the absurdity of how people were being treated, from the lens of a society that had begun to realize the issues that this industrialization caused.

Frederick Taylor was a staple in this movement. While he was not trying to cause any damage and his ideas were a fantastic theory, the ways those ideas caught on were less than satisfactory. It promoted this mandated working experience that gave employers new power, which they would soon abuse.

The problems we now attribute to industrialization were noticed back then too, but for so long we turned a blind eye. It’s difficult for us to understand how much we truly knew, and it is seldom taught. Frank Kimball’s lecture in the Bulletin of the Essex Institute is an amazing look into the issues that were really going on at the time and the extent to which scientists were aware. It’s astounding the impacts that industrialization had on civilization in such a short time.

Yet despite all this, as we know it today, nothing was really done. Most of these issues went unchecked for a long while and some still remain in our society.

Immigration

The goal of the immigration page is to show not only the impact that the new faces had on America but also the impact that America had on them. How they were treated and how they were used speaks volumes to the society of the time. The Unstoppable Boy is an example of immigrants’ pure desperation to enter the country.

Frank Leslie’s work aimed at the discrimination faced by some trying to enter. The Chinese were often looked down upon by Americans. Many thought they were taking jobs and ruining American lives. This growing disdain led to them being barred from entering the country. Leslie demonstrates how despite providing America with genuinely great things, they were cast out. America’s political enemies were all fine, but not the Chinese. It displays how unfair immigrants were treated.

“The New Colossus” featured on the statue of liberty is a great testimony to what America ‘symbolized’. They put on a show of liberty and freedom, being able to create a better life. In reality, this was not always true. These feelings were quickly retracted in the coming years and often left immigrants scrambling around.

The Gilded Age created a lasting impact on these immigrants and their families. Some of the class struggles faced today by minorities are largely due to the massive disdain created towards immigrants during this time.
